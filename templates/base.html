Every year close to about 126,000 people lose their lives due to skin melanoma. Most cases occur in countries exposed to high UV index throughout the year like United States, Australia, New Zealand, Norway, etc. Timely detection of a malignant case could same lives. Dermo is a AI powered skin melanoma detector that is highly accurate and could help assist Doctors and other medical workers detect an early sign of the disease.

Skin cancer is the most common cancer in the United States and worldwide.

  • 1 in 5 Americans will develop skin cancer by the age of 70.
  • More than 2 people die of skin cancer in the U.S. every hour.
  • Having 5 or more sunburns doubles your risk for melanoma.
  • When detected early, the 5-year survival rate for melanoma is 99 percent.



Ozone layer depletion

Ozone layer depletion increases the amount of UVB that reaches the Earth’s surface. Laboratory and epidemiological studies demonstrate that UVB causes non-melanoma skin cancer and plays a major role in malignant melanoma development.

  • It’s estimated that the number of new melanoma cases diagnosed in 2020 will increase by almost 2 percent.
  • South eastern countries like India and China emits huge amount of ozone depleting substances hence could be region of higher cases in future.
